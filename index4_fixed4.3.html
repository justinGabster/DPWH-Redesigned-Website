Philippines
Transparency Seal

Symbolism of the Transparency Seal

A pearl buried inside a tightly-shut shell is practically worthless. Government information is a pearl, meant to be shared with the public in order to maximize its inherent value.

The Transparency Seal, depicted by a pearl shining out of an open shell, is a symbol of a policy shift towards openness in access to government information. It hopes to inspire Filipinos in the civil service to be more open to citizen engagement and to invite the Filipino citizenry to exercise their right to participate in governance.

About the Atlas

Over the years, the Department of Public Works and Highways has delivered key infrastructure projects that unlock opportunities and support national development. The 2025 DPWH Infrastructure Atlas provides updated data on the condition and characteristics of the country's roads and bridges, covering national, regional, and district levels.

Scope & Coverage

It presents a ten-year overview (2015-2025), with bridge data traced back to 2007. The Atlas is divided into two volumes: Volume I (Roads) and Volume II (Bridges), featuring data through clear matrices and visualized using OpenStreet and National Geographic base maps.

Collaboration & Methodology

Recognizing the value of reliable infrastructure data, the DPWH — through the Statistics Division, Planning Service, and in collaboration with District Engineering Offices and Regional Offices — has strengthened efforts to deliver accurate and accessible information. Enhanced maps, graphs, and tables enable easier analysis, comparison, and understanding of the nation's infrastructure landscape.

Overview

Infrastructure development remains critical to sustaining economic growth, improving social living standards, and removing bottlenecks to international commerce. The administration recognizes PPP as a key approach to invest resources for adequate road infrastructure.

PPP describes a government service or private business venture which is funded, constructed and operated through a partnership between the government and the private sector. The private party provides a public service and assumes substantial financial, technical and operational risks.

Government contributions include capital subsidies, revenue subsidies, tax breaks, and guaranteed annual revenues. The PPP Service handles all PPP projects in the Department, guided by the JICA-assisted Preparatory Survey for PPP Infrastructure Development.

Existing Situation - Metro Manila

Arterial road network of 6 circumferential and 10 radial roads proposed in the late 1960s. All radial roads are completed; circumferential roads remain incomplete.

Three expressways (NLEX, SLEX with Skyway, Manila-Cavite Toll Expressway) function individually and are not yet formed as a network.

Heavy traffic is the norm in Metro Manila. Travel speed on most major roads is less than 20 km/hr, dropping below 10 km/hr on some roads including Aguinaldo Highway.

About DPWH

The Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) is the primary government agency responsible for the planning, design, construction, and maintenance of national infrastructure in the Philippines.

Mandate

The planning, design, construction and maintenance of infrastructure facilities, especially national highways, flood control and water resource development systems, and other public works in accordance with national development objectives.

Section 3, Executive Order No. 124, s. 1987

Vision

By 2040, DPWH is an excellent government agency, enabling a comfortable life for Filipinos through safe, reliable and resilient infrastructure.

Mission

To provide and manage quality infrastructure facilities and services responsive to the needs of the Filipino people in the pursuit of national development objectives.

Quality Policy

  • We commit to provide quality, safe, and environment-friendly infrastructure that improves the life of every Filipino.
  • We commit to comply with all requirements and continuously improve efficiency and effectiveness in serving the public.
  • We endeavour to implement the RIGHT PROJECTS at the RIGHT COST determined through transparent and competitive bidding; with the RIGHT QUALITY according to international standards; delivered ON TIME through close monitoring of project implementation; and carried out by the RIGHT PEOPLE who are competent and committed to uphold the values of public service, integrity, professionalism, excellence, and teamwork.

Core Values

Public Service Integrity Professionalism Excellence Teamwork

History of DPWH

The Department of Public Works and Highways traces its roots to the Spanish colonial era, evolving over four centuries into the infrastructure backbone of the modern Philippine government.

Background

The development of DPWH into its present structure underwent a long process of evolution spanning a century of colorful and significant events. The Department is considered as old as the Philippine government itself — its existence dates back about four centuries to the Spanish colonial era, when the first settlement roads were constructed by forced labor in 1565.

Key Milestones
1565 — First settlement roads
1867 — Chief of Public Works
1898 — Revolutionary Dept. of War & Public Works
1905 — Bureau of Public Works
1987 — DPWH formally established (E.O. 124)
Spanish Era
1565

First Settlement Roads

First roads constructed by forced labor under Spanish colonizers. Fortresses connected by improved trails serving as supply lines.

1867

Royal Decree — Chief of Public Works

The King of Spain designated the Spanish Governor General as Chief of Public Works, assisted by the Junta Consultiva.

1868

Bureau of Public Works & Bureau of Communications

The Bureau of Public Works and Highways (Obras Publicas) and Bureau of Communications were organized under a civil engineer known as Director General.

American Period & Early Republic
1898

Department of War and Public Works

Gen. Emilio Aguinaldo's Organic Decree of June 23 created four government departments, including the Department of War and Public Works, in the revolutionary government.

Philippine Revolutionary Government
1902

Department of Commerce and Police

Act Nos. 222 and 268 of the Philippine Commission created the Bureau of Engineering and Construction of Public Works and the Bureau of Architecture and Construction of Public Buildings under the Department of Commerce and Police.

American Colonial Period
Oct 26, 1905

Bureau of Public Works Established

A Reorganization Act created the Bureau of Public Works under the Department of Commerce and Police. Provincial boards were created in 1907 to finance construction of provincial roads and bridges.

Modern Era & Reorganization
1987

Executive Order No. 124 — DPWH Established

By virtue of Executive Order No. 124, dated January 30, 1987, the agency was formally reorganized as the Department of Public Works and Highways.

About the Logo

1
3
2
4
5

The design conveys the idea of Filipino engineering excellence with sub-themes such as the fine craftsmanship of Filipino design engineers, architects and the whole field of engineering profession, competence and integrity.

  1. Gear Frame — Completely circular, representing motion, transmission and direction towards the common man's economic and social upliftment. Seeks unity of effort, direction and purpose.
  2. Highway — Stretches northward and merges to infinity, conveying that roads complement public works projects hand-in-hand.
  3. Doric Column — Symbol of the building and construction industry, denoting unity of objectives and unity of efforts.
  4. Inner Circle — Encases all elements, representing the wholeness and completeness of the Department's mandate.
  5. Orange & White Lanes — Represent engineering outputs: roads and bridges, flood control, ports, water supply, national buildings, and other public works.

Thus, the doric column and the highway should be taken in a total context.

DPWH ATLAS

Compiled by the Statistics Division · Planning Service

Infrastructure is the foundation of a strong Republic. It facilitates economic activity, enhances mobility, improves living conditions and contributes to the achievement of social justice which are deemed key factors that will help maximize opportunities and realize potentials of the Filipino people. Through the years, the Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) has implemented various infrastructure projects under its manifold programs.

This 2025 version of the DPWH Infrastructure Atlas presents updated information on the current characteristics and condition of the country's vital infrastructures, specifically roads and bridges, on a national, regional and per district engineering office basis on a ten-year basis from CY 2015 to CY 2025 and from base year 2007 for bridges. It is comprised of two (2) parts each dedicated to providing detailed information on national roads (Volume I) and bridges (Volume II). Said information are condensed and laid out in matrices and through maps presented in Open Street and National Geographic World base maps for roads and bridges, respectively.

Being aware of the significant application of infrastructure statistics, the Department, through the Statistics Division, Planning Service with the participation of District Engineering Offices (DEOs) under the supervision of Regional Offices (ROs), has undertaken concerted effort to provide updated information on the current progress of national roads and bridges through the DPWH Atlas. Information contained herein is laid out in such a way as to provide users a clear idea on the infrastructure data being presented. Illustrations like maps, graphs, figures and tables were enhanced to easily identify and compare statistics.

As one of the DPWH's contributions in promoting the public's right to information which covers matters of public concern, this report presents road and bridge inventory data and information useful to planners, researchers, policy and decision-makers. It is the Department's ardent hope and desire that this work, modest as it may seem, will serve its purpose.

Road and Bridge Inventory

National Asset Registry · DPWH Bureau of Design & Planning Service

The Road and Bridge Inventory (RBI) is the official registry of all national roads and bridges under DPWH jurisdiction. It serves as the primary data source for infrastructure planning, maintenance programming, and budget allocation decisions across the country.

The inventory is maintained through the Road and Bridge Information Application (RBIA) system, institutionalized by Department Order No. 124, Series of 2016. The RBIA enables district engineering offices to continuously update road and bridge data, ensuring decision-makers have access to accurate, current information on the condition and extent of the national road network.

Road data is classified by surface type (concrete, asphalt, gravel, earth), jurisdiction, and condition rating. Bridge data is maintained through a Bridge Management System (BMS) with condition assessments conducted under a standardized five-type inspection protocol established by Department Order No. 43, Series of 2005.

The inventory is publicly accessible through the DPWH GIS-based RBI web application, which allows users to search and filter data by region, district engineering office, road classification, and condition status. StreaMS — Streamflow Management System

Bureau of Design · Water Projects Division

The Streamflow Management System (StreaMS) is the DPWH's online platform for collecting, processing, and managing streamflow data across the Philippines. It is maintained by the Bureau of Design — Water Projects Division (BOD-WPD) as part of the National Hydrologic Data Collection Program (NHDCP).

StreaMS provides accessible records and maps of river flow data sourced from stream gauging stations nationwide. Historical information on river flow is used to generate discharge measurements that allow engineers and water resources planners to design sustainable and resilient infrastructure. The system is instrumental in flood control and management, localized disaster risk planning, and water resources development plans throughout the country.

Records of streamflow are the fundamental data used in developing reliable surface water supplies, as they provide information on the availability of streamflow and its variability in time and space. These records are used both in the planning and design of surface-water related projects, and in the management or operation of such projects after they have been built.

StreaMS supports water resource regulation, flood control management, and disaster risk reduction at the local and national scale. It was developed with technical assistance from the USAID Be Secure Project and is expected to operate on a nationwide scale covering all river gauging stations across the country's major river basins.
